About

Web developer | London

I have recently finished a Web Development Immersive course at General Assembly London. It was an intensive 12 week course the took me from a beginner in HTML and JavaScript, to deploying Full-Stack applications built with tools such as React, MongoDB, Express and Node.js. The pace of new topics and content was fierce but I found it to be one of the most rewarding experiences of my life.

Previously, I worked for Micro-Power Electronics Ltd., a small electronics company that creates equipment for the power industry. I was tasked with creating a dashboard to show data from industrial sensors. Through my own learning and internal assistance, I was able to gain the skills necessary to integrate the data and create client side visualisations. I discovered I really enjoyed working in JavaScript and learning web technologies. This created a desire to formalise my training as a full stack developer and why I joined General Assembly.

I am looking for a workplace that can provide me with challenges and surround me with experienced colleagues who I can learn from. I want to be able to progress as a developer by learning new skills and by being given greater responsibilities.

Communication & Teamwork

Respect, Empathy, Listening, Conflict Management, Cooperation, Negotiating

When working on a group project at General Assembly, I found that members of the team wanted different levels of face to face communication, with some team members disturbing others whilst they were working hard and trying to concentrate. I opened a dialog to resolve the conflict, and by listening to everyone in the group with respect and empathy we came to a solution; regular scheduled meetings, so that people could feel included in the team but also allow those that needed their own space to thrive. This led to a much happier and more productive environment.

Adaptability, Problem Solving & Creativity

self-motivation, self-learning, persistence

Micro-Power Electronics creates electronic sensors for the power industry and was commissioned to create a device to collect sensor data and run an internal web server so that they could be configured and their internal logs be downloaded from a web browser. I researched online and followed tutorials to learn the basics of HTML and JavaScript and how to incorporate them into an embedded C project on the device’s PIC32 microcontroller. This self-motivation was crucial as during the project, I was often working on my own and would frequently hit problems. Through persistence, experimentation and, importantly, asking for help I was able to overcome these issues. The end result was a product that exceeded the clients expectations and changed the project from a system tool, to a product that can be used to generate customers.
